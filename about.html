The Interconnected Web

Nature is a balanced system where every organism, from the smallest bacteria to the blue whale, plays a specific role. Forests provide clean air, while oceans regulate our global temperature.

High biodiversity makes ecosystems more resilient, meaning they can recover faster from natural disasters or human impacts. If one species is removed, it can cause a "trophic cascade," affecting hundreds of other species in that environment.

One of the most exciting new areas of research is the study of the "Wood Wide Web," a vast underground network of fungi that allows trees to communicate, share nutrients, and even warn each other about pests!
